Before we go on with examples, let's take a look at this weird cases: Lative, Benefactive, and Locative with the Prepositional.

The Lative case is for the preposition "to", whatever noun or pronoun that comes after this preposition, will take the Primary Distinctive case, such as:

1. You are coming to me
In Irikad, the sentence would be: Siz meni daneriz
In most occasions, the verb (such as daneriz) would come at the end of the idea.

2. I am going to you
In Irikad, the sentence would be: Men sizi yanim

The Benefactive case is for the preposition "for", see here:

1. Is this for me?
In Irikad, this would be: Bu məni?
Note: Irikad has no conjugation for "to be" other than the common emphasis on that verb, then every pronoun would use the word ey. Such as: You ARE a man | Siz EY bir insa
Notice how the "insa" did not undergo any case-changes because of the article "bir" meaning "a/an".

2. Is that for him?
In Irikad: Budur oyəni?

You should get the point by now, it just follows the pattern. Locative covers the preposition "at".

The term Prepositional refers to the form of the pronoun/noun after every other preposition.

The Genitive case may be a little harder for learners to comprehend, as these are endings to a word, rather than separate words.

1. That is my house
In Irikad: Budur iyemın
Follow this rule for each genitive casing.



Section 2: Nouns

Nouns are split into 3 groups, CV-stem, C-stem, and V-stem nouns.
CV-stem nouns are when a noun ends in a consonant then a vowel, such as -re, -sa, etc.
C-stem nouns are when a noun ends in only a consonant, such as datətek (computer)
And V-stem nouns are usually only 2 to 3 letter words that only end in a vowel, such as the word su (water)

As you can see, wherever there needs to be a consonant there is either a y or an r, these alternate based on the vowel after it. So when the next vowel is either i, y, or ı, then the filler-consonant will be an r, and when the next vowel is any other vowel, then the filler-consonant will be a y.

Examples:

1. I want to drive to you
Irikad: Men domim sizi sereqırum

2. I am playing on my computer
Irikad: Men datətekənmınan klorim

Note: All prepositions are added AFTER the noun, as shown above, the "an" is after the rest of the noun in datətekənmın-AN. Split up, this would look like datətekən (Prepositional Singular) + -mın (First Person + Genitive Singular) + -an (Preposition "on")
