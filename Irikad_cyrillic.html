Before we go on with examples, let's take a look at this weird cases: Lative, Benefactive, and Locative with the Prepositional.

The Lative case is for the preposition "to", whatever noun or pronoun that comes after this preposition, will take the Primary Distinctive case, such as:

1. You are coming to me
In Irikad, the sentence would be: Сиз мени данериз
In most occasions, the verb (such as данериз) would come at the end of the idea.

2. I am going to you
In Irikad, the sentence would be: Мен сизи јаним

The Benefactive case is for the preposition "for", see here:

1. Is this for me?
In Irikad, this would be: Бу мәни?
Note: Irikad has no conjugation for "to be" other than the common emphasis on that verb, then every pronoun would use the word еј. Such as: You ARE a man | Сиз ЕЈ бир инса
Notice how the "инса" did not undergo any case-changes because of the article "бир" meaning "a/an".

2. Is that for him?
In Irikad: Будур ојәни?

You should get the point by now, it just follows the pattern. Locative covers the preposition "at".

The term Prepositional refers to the form of the pronoun/noun after every other preposition.

The Genitive case may be a little harder for learners to comprehend, as these are endings to a word, rather than separate words.

1. That is my house
In Irikad: Будур ијемын
Follow this rule for each genitive casing.



Section 2: Nouns

Nouns are split into 3 groups, CV-stem, C-stem, and V-stem nouns.
CV-stem nouns are when a noun ends in a consonant then a vowel, such as -re, -sa, etc.
C-stem nouns are when a noun ends in only a consonant, such as датәтек (computer)
And V-stem nouns are usually only 2 to 3 letter words that only end in a vowel, such as the word су (water)

As you can see, wherever there needs to be a consonant there is either a ј or an р, these alternate based on the vowel after it. So when the next vowel is either и, ј, or ы, then the filler-consonant will be an р, and when the next vowel is any other vowel, then the filler-consonant will be a ј.

Section 3: Verbs

All verbs have a variety of conjugations, they all follow the same rules and are always the same for each verb, no matter what kind of verb it is.

As you can see, the infinitive of each verb always ends in -ырум

Examples:

1. I want to drive to you
Irikad: Мен домим сизи серегырум

2. I am playing on my computer
Irikad: Мен датәтекәнмынан клорим

Note: All prepositions are added AFTER the noun, as shown above, the "ан" is after the rest of the noun in датәтекәнмын-АН. Split up, this would look like датәтекән (Prepositional Singular) + -мын (First Person + Genitive Singular) + -ан (Preposition "on")
